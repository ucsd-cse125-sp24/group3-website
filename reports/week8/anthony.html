What goals were you able to accomplish?

  • I was able to load in the wall textures Coco made. They look awesome! I spent some time playing around with the lighting and we settled on a turquoise blue-ish color. I also loaded in the pillar texture she made which is placed between walls around the maze.

  • I made an FPS counter that gets rendered to the top left of the window. This became more important as all the point lights and other additions meant the game was not always responsive and buttery smooth anymore.

  • I loaded in the Sun God model and modified the fireball trap to shoot from the sun god’s beak. I also changed the trap to only shoot at the player if they are within line of sight of the sun gods (within a cone coming out of the sun god’s beak). This means that the player can try to sneak around the sun god to avoid getting hit. I also worked with Tyler to squash some bugs in getting the projectiles to behave correctly.

  • I moved our rendering pipeline to use deferred shading (almost). This was motivated by the poor performance from the addition of the torch lights. In the previous version, for every mesh being rendered we had to send lighting information to the GPU. Even with a hardcoded number of 16 light sources this was a bottleneck and FPS would hover between 20-40 on various machines. Instead deferred shading will loop through every object and gather geometry data. Then, we loop through all the pixels on the screen and shade each one accordingly. Another 125 group mentioned how they had success with it and recommended us to try it out. I have an initial implementation that only renders walls and the frame rate is much higher (140fps on my machine) even with large mazes that don’t have any of the optimizations that we previously had (ex: not rendering far away objects). I still need to add back rendering for all the other non-wall/floor objects and fix the in-game UI which broke.
